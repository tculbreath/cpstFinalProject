Pregnancy By Trimester

First Trimester

First Trimester

Month 1

Did the first month of pregnancy slip by you? Don’t worry this is often the case, especially for women who weren’t intending to conceive. The first month of pregnancy does come with some excitement though.

Week 1

Now is the time to start taking prenatal vitamins and quit your bad habits. You aren’t technically pregnant, but you will be soon. Get prepared if you are planning for a baby.

For more information about your first week of pregnancy visit the line below

Week One

Week 2

This is the week where ovulation happens. Ovulation is the best chance of getting pregnant. If you are thinking about getting pregnant aim to have sex one to two days before your expected ovulation date. If you are planning on getting pregnant, then now would make a great time to find a midwife or obstetrician.

For more information about your second week of pregnancy visit the line below

Week Two

Week 3

At this stage of pregnancy, you will not be showing any symptoms. Check with your doctor about any medication you are current taking. Get your flu shot if you have not already.

For more information about your third week of pregnancy visit the line below

Week Three

Week 4

This is the first week that you would be able to get a positive pregnancy test result!! Congrats. Symptoms may start rolling in. Anything from feeling tired to having sore breast. No need to panic, these along with the increasing need to visit the bathroom are all normal. Spend your time now invest in supportive bras and avoiding secondhand smoke.

For more information about your forth week of pregnancy visit the line below

Week Four

Month 2

The second month of pregnancy brings your symptoms into full swing. You still have a long way to go with seven months of your pregnancy left. However, the first trimester is the worst of it. Make it through this part and it’ll be a breeze.

Week 5

Pregnancy brain is real! Even though you baby is about the size of a point of a pen the effects he has on your body are very real. Try different memory techniques to help you remember things through the pregnancy. Also, now is a great time to set up your first appointment

Week Five

Week 6

During week 6 of pregnancy, it is a good time to remind yourself to stop risky behaviors. Even cutting down on caffeine can help make your pregnancy successful. Give some time to think about when you want to tell your boss about your pregnancy.

Week Six

Week 7

This week your baby will double in size, but he still is very small. You may notice an uptick in your cravings as well. Weight gain should not be significant in your first trimester so take it easy.

Week Seven

Week 8

During this week you may get the chance to listen to your baby’s heartbeat. Yes, you due date is far away, and we are not even out of the third trimester yet, but now is a good time to start reading up on babies.

Week Eight

Month 3

By the end of this month your baby will have all its arms, legs, fingers and what nots. While your baby’s gender is determined it is hard at this stage to pick it up from an ultrasound.

Week 9

Exercises like Kegels can help you build your strength for you upcoming delivery. It is best to start preparing now.

Week Nine

Week 10

Congrats! Your baby is now considered a fetus! Your pregnancy symptoms should start to subside now. If you are up in age (over 35) give genetic testing some consideration.

Week Ten

Week 11

Be prepared for weird food cravings. They could reflect a medical need for you and your baby. If you are experiencing these, please contact your doctor.

Week Eleven

Week 12

You may be starting to show at this stage. Your uterus, which will grow 1,000 times larger over the course of the pregnancy.

Week Twelve

Second Trimester

Second Trimester

Month 4

This is the first month of your second trimester. Hopefully, you are like the majority of women who experience a return of their appetite.

Week 13

Again, congrats on finishing your first trimester. You can start shopping for clothes. The extra 300 calories your aiming for will certainly help you earn them.

Week Thirteen

Week 14

Hopefully now, with morning sickness being a thing of the past, you have a renewed sense of energy. Check out some local maternity classes to help make the time until the baby arrives move faster.

Week Fourteen

Week 15

Be sure to get any time sensitive testing done this week. The window begins to close on a lot of important testing to help you find out about any potential abnormalities.

Week Fifteen

Week 16

Be on the lookout. Now and for the next few weeks expect to feel your baby move. Also, make a decision on whether or not you want to know the gender.

Week Sixteen

Month 5

Congrats Mom and Dad! Your almost at your midway point. In about another 4 months’ time you’ll have your baby.

Week 17

Have you started having abnormal dreams? That’s perfectly normal. The stress of an upcoming pregnancy often leads to odd dreams.

Week Seventeen

Week 18

The more room the baby takes up the less room there is for your bladder. Make no excuses for your increased trips to the bathroom.

Week Eighteen

Week 19

Since your symptoms have subsided, try planning a babymoon with your spouse. Quality time for you and him

Week Nineteen

Week 20

Your urge next may overwhelm you. Don’t let it tucker you out. Anything that will take time in preparing start now, so it is out of the way before the baby gets here.

Week Twenty

Month 6

By the end of your sixth month your baby will have finger and toe prints. Even better, those dreamy little eyes you will look at begin to open.

Week 21

Gestational diabetes can set in in this week. However, it won’t appear until much later in your pregnancy. Notice something weird? Reach out to your doctor immediately.

Week Twenty-One

Week 22

Your baby is almost an entire pound. His additional weight may make it harder to go to the bathroom comfortably. To help with this try to increase your intake of water and fiber.

Week Twenty-Two

Week 23

I hope you got your babymoon in. The time for you to take long distance trips in your baby moon is about to expire. The closer your due date gets the more likely your doctor is to advise against it.

Week Twenty-Three

Week 24

Schedule a glucose testing. No, it isn’t fun. It involves a super thick sweet liquid that you have to manage to keep down and needles. Plus, the aftermath usually has your baby jumping around for the rest of the day.

Week Twenty-Four

Third Trimester

Third Trimester

Month 7

Now your baby is moving because he is being stimulated. Things like sounds, light and sadly even pain have baby moving around much more frequently.

Week 25

Notice any heartburn? How about leg cramps. As the baby grows all of this puts an inconvenience on your digestive system and supporting limbs.

Week Twenty-Five

Week 26

As time ticks by there are more and more things to check off your to-do list. May sure you investigate the needs of you baby for after he arrives. Good daycares often have waiting list. Also, now is a good time to start dwindling down the baby names.

Week Twenty-Six

Week 27

Don’t worry about your weight. The baby and the contents of your uterus have a big effect on the numbers on the scale. Keep telling yourself it’s the baby.

Week Twenty-Seven

Week 28

What is less painful than contractions but just as annoying?...You guess it! Braxton Hicks. They usually kick into gear this week. However, trust there is a difference between them and the real thing.

Week Twenty-Eight

Month 8

You’ve almost made it! One more month to go until you’re with your baby. Wrap up any events like baby showers now, before you’re too uncomfortable to attend.

Week 29

Your baby is the size of a basketball now. For your comfort, try to stand up as frequently as possible in these last few weeks. When eating gets to be a hassle try smaller meals throughout the day.

Week Twenty-Nine

Week 30

As your uterus crowds almost everything inside you, you may find it harder to catch your breath. Wrap up any last-minute things for legal changes involving the baby.

Week Thirty

Week 31

As the space within you gets tight you will notice your baby’s kick lose their umph! All of this is very normal at this stage.

Week Thirty-One

Week 32

Feeling yourself losing you glow. Hurry! Throw your baby shower now before you lose all of your momentum.

Week Thirty-Two

Month 9

Yay you have made it to what for most is their final month of pregnancy! Congrats you’ll soon be delivering a healthy baby.

Week 33

One in three women have a C-section so don’t be oppose to one if it becomes necessary. Make a wish list for your due date but try your best not to get attached to it.

Week Thirty-Three

Week 34

Stop! The house will never be clean enough for your new arrival. Take some time to take your car seat up to the fire department. They’ll install it for you no problem.

Week Thirty-Four

Week 35

Now is a great time to choose a pediatrician.

Week Thirty-Five

Week 36

Your baby may choose now to drop. This is make you more comfortable because you’ll be able to breathe.

Week Thirty-Six

Beyond Full Term

They don't give extra credit for going past this point

Week 37

You may start leaking your breastmilk. It doesn’t mean anything is wrong if it is or isn’t some women don’t start lactating until after their baby is born.

Week Thirty-Seven

Week 38

Congrats! Your pregnancy is considered full term now. Start packing your hospital bag if you haven’t already.

Week Thirty-Eight

Week 39

Don’t feel bad taking your maternity leave early. You’ll need and deserve the rest soon enough.

Week Thirty-Nine

Week 40

You will deliver soon. If not, your OB will induce you.
